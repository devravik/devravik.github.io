# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Homeowners with domestic staff** (primary): tracking maids, cooks, drivers. Often managing 2-4 people, paying in cash, keeping mental records. Not tech-averse but not power users. Uses the app daily, briefly.

**Small business owners**: shop owners, delivery ops, warehouse managers with 5-30 employees. Need attendance + salary records without expensive payroll software.

**Tutors and coaching center owners**: tracking 20-100 students across batches. Need bulk attendance and fee collection, not a complex LMS.

**Landlords**: managing 2-10 rental units. Want to track rent received, not received, and security deposits. Currently using notebooks or WhatsApp.

**Geographic context**: Primary India (INR default, household help culture, coaching centers). Global via 9-currency support.

**Device context**: Android-first, used in brief daily bursts (marking attendance, logging a payment). Discovers via Play Store search or word of mouth.

## Product Purpose

AttendAssist is a free Android app that replaces the paper register and the spreadsheet for people managing staff, tenants, or students. It stores everything locally - no account, no cloud, no subscription - while covering four management contexts (household, business, classroom, rental) that every other app handles separately.

Success: a visitor reads the landing page, immediately recognizes their specific use case (maid tracker, rent collector, student roll), and downloads the app with confidence that it will handle their exact problem without hassle or hidden cost.

## Brand Personality

**Warm professional.** Reliable without being stiff. Competent without being corporate. The personality of a well-organized friend who knows what they're doing.

Three words: **Specific. Trustworthy. Calm.**

## Anti-references

**Generic SaaS landing page**: floating blob gradients, "Transform your workflow" hero copy, identical feature-icon grids, glassy cards on dark gradients. Feels impersonal, over-designed, and aimed at nobody specific.

**Enterprise HR software** (SAP, ADP, Keka, GreytHR): heavy, grey, cluttered with compliance jargon. Implies costs, contracts, IT departments. The exact opposite of "free, no account, works offline."

**Aggressive consumer fintech** (PhonePe, Groww campaign pages): celebration gradients, neon accents, confetti, "YOU COULD WIN" energy. Trust-eroding for an app that promises privacy.

## Design Principles

1. **Name the person, not the category.** "Track your maid's attendance" outperforms "manage your workforce." Specificity is the differentiator; generic language collapses the advantage.

2. **Privacy is the headline, not the footnote.** "No account. No cloud. No tracking." is an unusual claim in a world of SaaS. It belongs up front, stated plainly, not buried in an FAQ.

3. **Show the actual app.** Screenshots with feature labels beat icons-and-text cards. Users deciding to download need to see the interface they'll live with.

4. **Free means free.** No asterisks, no "free tier," no "basic plan." When free is unconditional, say it unconditionally. Don't soften it.

5. **Consistent quality across all four pillars.** Household staff, payroll, rent, and classroom each deserve equal visual weight. No pillar should feel like an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast as a floor. Touch targets 44x44px minimum (primary audience on mobile). No animation that can't be disabled for reduced-motion. Body text minimum 16px. Comfortable line length (60-70ch) for readability in Indian English.
